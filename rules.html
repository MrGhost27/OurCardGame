Section 01

Overview

Sniper is a tactical card game for 2 or more players. Each player is a sniper operating in the field, drawing cards from a shared deck and using them to locate, attack, and eliminate their opponents.

Cards are held privately in hand and revealed only when played. The last player standing wins.

The golden rule: You cannot attack an enemy you haven't found. Before any attack, you must first establish that you know where your target is.
🃏
Section 02

Taking a Turn

On your turn, draw one card from the top of the deck. It is added to your hand privately — other players cannot see what you drew.

You may then choose to play cards from your hand. Playing a card means displaying it to all other players using the Display Cards feature. Cards are played in response to situations or to initiate an attack — there is no fixed "play phase".

Holding cards: You are never forced to play a card immediately after drawing it. Cards can be saved in hand and played at any time, including during another player's turn in response to an attack.

After drawing, play passes to the next player in the turn order shown at the top of the screen.

Finding an Enemy

Before attacking a player, you must establish their location using a Found card or a successful High Ground roll. Announce which player you are targeting when you play a Found card.

Once a Found card (or successful High Ground) has been played, you may immediately follow it with attack cards in the same display action, or display them separately.

Section 05

Defending & Counter-Attacking

When you are attacked, you have a window to respond before the attack resolves. Responses are played by displaying cards from your hand.

Decoy
Play in response to any attack. The attack misses entirely — nothing happens to you.
Defence
Decoy deployed
Play in response to any attack. The attack misses entirely — nothing happens to you.
Defence
Relocate
Play in response to a Grenade to cancel its effect. Must be played within 2 turns of the Grenade being played.
Move
Counter Snipe: 4+ (D6)
If you deflected an attack with a Decoy, you may shoot back. Roll a D6 — on a 4 or higher, the counter-attack lands as a headshot: instant elimination.
Counter
🔄 Counter-Attack Sequence
1
You are attacked and play Decoy — the attack misses
2
If you hold Counter Snipe, you may now display it and roll a D6
3
On a 4 or higher — the counter-attack is a headshot. The original attacker is instantly eliminated, regardless of their health
4
The attacker may play Decoy or Decoy deployed to cancel the counter-attack
5
On a 3 or lower, or if the attacker plays a Decoy — the counter misses
⭐ The hole in one: A successful Counter Snipe is the rarest and most celebrated play in the game. Not only did you survive an attack, you turned it into an instant elimination. Difficult to pull off — a Decoy card stops it entirely — but devastating when it lands.
Grenade & Relocate: A Grenade starts counting down the moment it is played. The targeted player has 2 turns (their own turns) to display a Relocate card. If they do, the Grenade fizzles. If the 2 turns pass without a Relocate being played, the player takes a wound.

Section 06

Health & Elimination

Each player has two health points. Health is currently tracked by the players themselves — keep track of your own wounds.

Healthy
Full health. Can take one wound before becoming critical.
Wounded
One wound taken. A second wound or a headshot will eliminate you.
Eliminated
Two wounds, or one headshot. You remain in the game as an observer.
Bandage
Play at any time to remove your Wounded status. You return to full health.
Heal
Headshot: An attack using Found him: Head + a Hit card is an instant elimination, regardless of current health. There is no defence beyond a Decoy card.

An eliminated player remains in the game as a spectator. They can see all displayed cards and watch the game continue, but cannot draw cards or take actions. Their turns are skipped automatically.

When only one player remains uneliminated, that player wins.

Section 08

Reshuffle

After any attack sequence has been fully resolved — whether the attack landed, was deflected, or a counter-attack played out — if two or more players remain uneliminated, the deck is reshuffled.

Attack resolves
Hit, Grenade, Counter Snipe, or all defences exhausted
Check player count
If only one player remains uneliminated — that player wins
Reshuffle the deck
All cards return to the deck. All hands are cleared. A new first player is chosen
Play continues
The chosen player draws first from the fresh deck
Manual reshuffle: The host can also trigger a reshuffle at any time using the Reshuffle All button if all players agree — for example if the deck runs empty before an attack is completed.
